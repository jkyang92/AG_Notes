\section{Lecture 18 (Matthias Zach): Tate resolutions over projective space}\label{lec:18-tate-resolutions-PPn}

Let $S = \k[x_0, \dots, x_n]$, $\Z$-graded with $\deg(x_i) = 1$, and let $E = \bigwedge_\k(e_0, \dots, e_n)$ be the exterior algebra with $\deg(e_i) = -1$. Let $E^*$ denote the $\k$-dual of $E$. We saw on Monday that there is an equivalence of categories
$
\RR \co D^b(S) \xra{\simeq} D^b(E)
$
that sends a finitely generated module $M$ (thought of as a complex concentrated in homological degree 0) to
\begin{equation}
\label{eqn:RMformula}
\RR(M) = \left[\cdots \xla{\del_\RR} E^*(i) \otimes_\k M_{-i} \xla{\del_\RR} E^*(i + 1) \otimes_\k M_{-i - 1} \xla{\del_\RR} \cdots \right],
\end{equation}
where $E^*(i) \otimes_\k M_{-i}$ is in homological degree $-i$. Moreover, we saw that we get an induced equivalence
\begin{equation}
\label{eqn:geobgg}
\overline{\RR} \co D^b(\PP^n) \xra{\simeq} D^{\on{sing}}(E) \ce B \co D^b(E) / \Perf(E),
\end{equation}
where $D^{\on{sing}}(E)$ denotes the singularity category of $E$, and $\Perf(E)$ is the category of perfect complexes in $E$. Recall that the objects in $D^{\on{sing}}(E)$ are the same as those in $D^b(E)$, but the morphisms are obtained by inverting all morphisms in $D^b(E)$ whose mapping cone lies in $\Perf(E)$. The equivalence~\eqref{eqn:geobgg} gives a ``geometric" version of the BGG correspondence, though an unsatisfying one: the passage from $D^b(S)$ to $D^b(\PP^n)$ translates to the passage from $D^b(E)$ to $D^{\on{sing}}(E)$, which is only visible at the level of morphisms. Since the morphisms in $D^{\on{sing}}(E)$ are essentially unknowable, this renders the projective geometry in $D^{\on{sing}}(E)$ effectively invisible. This problem is solved by Eisenbud-Fl\o ystad-Schreyer's theory of Tate resolutions~\cite{EFS}. The goal of this lecture is to explain this theory and gesture toward some of its applications.

Conceptually, we need a way to get our hands on the morphisms in $D^{\on{sing}}(E)$. There is a classical method for making the morphisms in a derived category explicit, namely by replacing it with an appropriate homotopy category. For instance, $D^b(E)$ is equivalent to the homotopy category $K^-(E)$ of complexes of finitely generated graded free $E$-modules whose terms vanish in large homological degrees. In the case of an exterior algebra, this equivalence may be given by taking the $\k$-dual of a minimal free resolution\footnote{It makes sense to take a (minimal) free resolution of any complex of finitely generated $E$-modules with bounded homology~\cite{Rob98}*{Proposition 4.4.1}.}. There is a similar result for the singularity category, due to Buchweitz~\cite{buc21}*{Theorem 4.4.1}, and this is the added ingredient we need. Specifically: there is an equivalence
\begin{equation}
\label{eqn:buchweitz}
D^{\on{sing}}(E) \xra{\simeq} K(E),
\end{equation}
where $K(E)$ is the homotopy category of (possibly unbounded on both sides) exact complexes of finitely generated graded free $E$-modules. This equivalence sends an object $C$ in $D^{\on{sing}}(E)$ to the mapping cone of the canonical quasi-isomorphism $f \co G \to F$, where $G$ is the minimal free resolution of the $\k$-dual of $N$, and $F$ is the $\k$-dual of the minimal free resolution of~$N$. We arrive at the following key commutative diagram:
\begin{equation}
\label{eqn:tatediagram}
\xymatrix{
D^b(S) \ar[d] \ar[r]_-{\simeq}^-{\RR} & D^b(E) \ar[d] \ar[r]_-{\simeq} & K^-(E) \ar[d] \\
D^b(\PP^n) \ar[r]^-{\overline{\RR}}_-{\simeq} & D^{\on{sing}}(E) \ar[r]_-{\simeq}^B & K(E). \\
}
\end{equation}
The only functor here we have not yet described is the rightmost vertical one, which sends an object~$F$ to $\cone(G \xra{\simeq} F)$, where $G$ is the minimal free resolution of~$F$.

With all this in mind, let us now give an alternative formulation of the BGG correspondence for~$\PP^n$. Essentially, it is given by composing the functors in the bottom row of Diagram~\eqref{eqn:tatediagram}. To obtain an explicit formula for this composition, we proceed as follows. Let $\F$ be a coherent sheaf on $\PP^n$ of Castelnuovo-Mumford regularity $r$, and let $M$ be a finitely generated graded $S$-module such that $\widetilde{M} = \F$. Let $G$ be the minimal free resolution of $\RR(M_{\ge r+1})$, and let $T(\F)$ be the mapping cone of the quasi-isomorphism $G \xra{\simeq} \RR(M)$. 

\begin{defn}[\cite{EFS}*{Section 4}]
The complex $T(\F)$ is called the \emph{Tate resolution of $\F$.} 
\end{defn}

\begin{prop}
\label{prop:tateBR}
For any coherent sheaf $\F$ on $\PP^n$, we have $T(\F) = (B \circ \overline{\RR})(\F)$.
\end{prop}

In other words, the Tate resolution functor is a model for the BGG correspondence for $\PP^n$. 

\begin{proof}
This follows from the commutativity of Diagram~\eqref{eqn:tatediagram}: the formula for $T$ lifts $\F$ to $D^b(S)$ and then applies the top two horizontal and right most vertical functors in the diagram. 
\end{proof}

The following is one of the main theorems of \cite{EFS}: 

\begin{thm}
\label{thm:tate1}
Let $\F$ be a coherent sheaf on $\PP^n$. Its Tate resolution $T(\F)$ is minimal, and for all $i, j \in \Z$, there is an isomorphism $T(\F)_i \cong \bigoplus_{j \in \Z} E^*(i + j) \otimes_\k H^j(\PP^n, \FF(-i - j))$.
\end{thm}

Theorem~\ref{thm:tate1} tells us how to actually see projective geometry via BGG: the terms of the Tate resolution of~$\F$ encode the sheaf cohomology of all the twists of $\F$. That is, the Buchweitz equivalence~\eqref{eqn:buchweitz} pulls the geometry out of the morphisms in the singularity category and expresses  it via the objects of $K(E)$.
